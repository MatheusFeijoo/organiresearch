Circular Economy

Sustainable Improvement group develops models and tools that help companies to contribute to sustainable development, promoting the progressive integration of circularity throughout the business model with triple impact objectives: economic, environmental and social. These models and tools are developed at both strategic and operational level (the latter following a Lean phliosophy based on data, Lean-Six Sigma). We are currently working on:

  • Development of methodologies for the implementation of industrial symbiosis to facliitate the adoption of circular economy principles.
  • Tools and techniques for the implementation of the Circular Economy in SMEs.
  • Strategies for the deployment and evaluation of sustainable and circular production.
  • Telemetry and diagnosis of water consumption in production processes.
  • Data science projects in the field of industrial process improvement.

Resilience

The Tecnun Resilience team focuses on developing more resilient and sustainable urban environments. Its main projects have focused on the capacities of cities to cope with both foreseen and unforeseen crises arising from climate change, the interdependence of critical infrastructures, social dynamics and cyber attacks. Group model building (GMB), system dynamics, Delphi Study and focus group techniques are used. In recent years, participation in both European and local projects has led research to focus mainly on climate change and cybersecurity issues. At Tecnun Resilience we are currently working on:

  • Analysis of social resilience specifying how interactions between authorities and emergency services with society need to be in order to engage society in disaster management and to better manage and respond to disasters.
  • Assessment and management models for resilience building in urban environments to better prepare for, respond to and recover from crises arising from climate change, interdependence of critical infrastructure and social dynamics.
  • Development of new models of governance in the urban environment in the context of climate change.
  • Frameworks for managing public-private and people partnerships to better implement resilience strategies in cities.
  • Management models to develop the awareness of the agents that make up a city to better implement resilience strategies on climate change issues.
  • Development of cyber resilience in companies to better prepare for, respond to and recover from cyber-attacks.

Mentor

The MENTOR group (Methodologies in Education and New Technologies Orientation and Research) focuses its research on the analysis, development and application of new technologies and methodologies to improve the learning process. It arises from Tecnun's interest in researching the Teaching-Learning Process and the advantages that Technological Innovation can provide to this process. Its milestones include the development of computer tools to support the learning process and various funded projects for the development and application of tools and material for innovation in learning. We are currently working on:

  • Development of material and methodologies for mastering learning in Mathematics
  • Development of material and methodologies for mastering learning in Computer Science
  • Study of the application of Flipped Learning in engineering higher education
  • Study of continuous assessment in STEM
